Miscellaneous lumps

Theory Examination Presentation Examples

Theory

Small lumps and bumps are common short cases. The objctive is to do a methodical examination and present the findings in a rational manner. This is a general discussion on the examination of miscellaneous lumps. What componet to examine depends on where the lump is. Use a bit of common sense without mechanically following all the steps. Do not listen for a bruit in what looks like a hydrocele or look for reducibility in what looks like a fibroademoma!

Components of the examinaton may be done in any order. Make sure that you have a clear idea about the lump before presenting the findings.

Start by introducing yourself, getting the consent, and adequately exposing the lump. Ask about pain before starting palpation.

The order of examinatin and order of presentation is not necesserily the same. Examine the lump to find the following

  • Site
  • Size
  • Shape
  • Surface
  • Contour
  • Consistancey
  • Pulsatility
  • Reducibility
  • Fluctuation
  • Mobility
  • Tenderness
  • Temperature
  • Transillumination
  • Percussion
  • Auscultation
When presenting, follow the SPACESPIT sequence.
  • Size
  • Position
  • Attachments
  • Consistancey
  • Edges
  • Surface and shape
  • Pulsation, thrills and bruits
  • Inflammation
  • Transillumination

The regional lymph nodes should be examined for enlargement

Examination

Site

Determine the anatomical location of the lump. Can be expressed as a well demarcated area such as left popletial fossa or describe with distances to fixed bony prominences. Keep in mind that body is three dimentional. Determine the depth as well. Depth can be expressed with the tissue plane such as subcutanneous. More informatin on the tissue plate is obtained after examining mobility

Size

An estimated for size is usually sufficient. Ask for any recent rapid enlargement in the history.

Shape

Not very helpful in diagnosis as almost all lumps will be spherical. Still it is added for completeness

Surface

Surface appearance can be smooth/rough, flat/raised, regular/irregular. Look for ulceration and necrosis.Color of the lump can be red if inflammed, black if necrosed.

Contour

Can be regualr or irregular

Consistancy

Describe as hard, firm, or soft.

Pulsatility

Pulsations can be transmitted or be intinsic pulsations. To diferentiate look for expansile pulsation.

Reducibility

Important when assessing hernial lumps. Compressiblity should be looked for in vericose veins

Fluctuation

Fluctuation indicates the presence of fluid in the lump. Fluid thrill can be elicited for larger lumps

Mobility

Very important to tell the tissue plane, attachments and the differential diagnosis. Lesions superficial to a muscle shoul be tested with the muscle relaxed and then contracted. If a lump that is mobile is fixed when muscle is contracted, the lump is attached to the muscle. Tethering can be demonstrated by moving the lump in two perpendicular planes as done for ganglions

Tendeness

Ask for tenderness before palpation

Temperature

To look for inflammation

Transilluination

Cysts will transilluinate. Lipomas and even hydrocehalus may be transilluminant in sufficiently bright light

Percussion

Limited use in small lumps. Hyperresonant abdominal masses indicate gas filled swellings

Auscultation

to look for vascular lesions.

Present the findings

When presenting the examination findings, tell the size, position, attachments, consistancy, edges, surface (and shape), pulsations (and thrils, bruits), signs of infalmmation (redness, warmth and tenderness), and transillumination.

Comment on the regional lymph nodes when it is likely to be a malignant lesion or inflammatory lesion. Metastasis in to lymph nodes will cause firm or hard painless lymphnodes. Inflammation causes reactive lymphadenopathy with soft tender lymph nodes.

Example

Example 1
Size2X2X2 cm
PositionMedial aspect of right wrist, in midline
AttachmentsTo deep tissue.
ConsistancyFirm
EdgesRegular
Surface and shapeSmooth
PulsationAbsent. Auscultation not done
InflammationNo
TransilluminationYes

There is a painless firm spherical lump about 2x2x2 cm on the inner aspect of the right wrist on the midline. The edges are regualr with a smooth surface. It is transilluminat. Lump is more mobile along the long axis of the hand than the short axis. (Diagnosis is ganglion cyst)

Example 2
Size6X6X3 cm
PositionBack, on the medial edge of the left scapula
AttachmentsNone
ConsistancySoft
EdgesRegular
Surface and shapeSmooth, red, discharging pus
PulsationAbsent. Auscultation not done
InflammationYes. Tender, warm and red
TransilluminationYes

There is a tender soft lump about 6X6X3 cm on the back of the chest wall at the medial edge of the left scapula. Edges are smooth and regular with a red smooth and shiney surface with a punctum at the center producing a purullent discharge. Lump is not attached to deep tissues. It is transilluminant.(Diagnosis is infected ruptured sebacious cyst)
